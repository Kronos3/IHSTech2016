IHS Technology

Student Projects

By far the most compelling STEM projects done at Ithaca High School happen in the Engineering Design and Development course, where students begin the year working in teams to build a boat from cardboard and one roll of duct tape to sail across the school’s pool. Most teams make it across. But the real fun starts later: teams of students come up with proposals for something to build throughout the year. At a banquet attended by the Ithaca City School District Superintendent Luvelle Brown, technology teachers, and representatives from nearby Cornell University, Ithaca College, and the Tompkins Cortland Community College, the teams present their ideas in an attempt to garner a fraction of the $2,000 alloted to the class. Having gotten funding, they begin work on their project.

Ithaca High School Technology Student Association president Zak Stillman’s team is working on a controller for new electric motors they want to install in Ithaca High School’s small Electrathon car. They want to replace the current brushless motor with a brushed one, but not pay the prohibitive cost of brushed motor controllers. This means building three circuit boards.

Other groups are converting a VW Buggy to run on propane, designing a device to be placed in a stream to generate electricity for hikers, creating a glove that will reduce sweat while gaming, and another is designing an app that can play many different card and board games in the app.
